Part A · Pre-Game Orientation

Before you open SuperbMarket…

Take two minutes to read the points below. They will focus your attention so you get the most out of playing today.

  • ①
    Watch your emotions as Jo. Notice what you feel at each decision point — not just whether a choice feels right or wrong, but what emotion is driving that feeling. Name it mentally.
  • ②
    Stay curious about your employee. Try to hold two questions in mind while you play: What is Lee feeling right now? and Why might they be behaving this way? Resist your first answer and look for a second.
  • ③
    Remember the BIW framework. After you play, you will be asked to name your emotions, rate their intensity (1–10), and write about them. The richer your in-game attention, the deeper your reflection will be.

Flag Your Transfer Scenario

Part C will ask you to reflect on a real-life interpersonal situation from the past two weeks. Before you start playing, briefly note what that situation is so it stays in mind.

Reflecting on a Key Decision

Think back to a decision point you just encountered in SuperbMarket — for example, how Jo responded to Lee arriving late, or how Jo handled the breakroom conversation. Pick the moment that felt most emotionally charged for you.

Tip: Use the Emotion Word Bank to push past surface-level labels. "Frustrated" becomes richer when you also identify "disrespected," "helpless," or "conflicted" beneath it.

Writing tip: Don't aim for perfect sentences — go where your mind takes you. Weave in emotion names with intensity ratings as you write (e.g., "I felt guilty (8) because…"). Aim for at least 60–80 words overall.
